Inner Product Space

25, Nov 2020

This week, I am studying Fourier transform and distributions. It takes me days to understand the relation between Fourier transform and distributions. Like most students, I studied how to do Fourier transform with the following formula: $$\begin{align}\hat{f}(s) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(x) e^{-2\pi i s x} d x , \ \ \ s \in \mathbb{R}\end{align}$$ I view Fourier transform as the continuous counterpart of Fourier coefficient: $$c_n = \frac{1}{L} \int_{a}^b f(x) e^{-2\pi i n x/L} dx, \ \ \ n \in \mathbb{Z}$$ According to Prof. Brad Osgood from Standford University, there is nothing wrong to understand Fourier transform in this way. However, I am complete lost later when he introduced concept of tempered distribution, which is a continuous linear functional on a space of test functions (Osgood, 2008 Online).

Trying to get the gist of tempered distribution, I did some research by searching and reading more notes. Thanks to the thesis written by Terri Joan Harris (2015), I could realize that tempered distribution is a subject of functional analysis, which is concerned with infinite-dimensional spaces.
