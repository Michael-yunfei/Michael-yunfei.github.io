Introduction

According to Stein (2011), it is better to start the learning of analysis with Fourier analysis as its ideas permeate much of the present-day analysis. I followed his path and devote myself on this subject by exploring some basic facts about Fourier series and Fourier transforms. My purpose is to understand the convergence of Fourier series. Since Fourier analysis plays a central role in analasis, I belive it would be helpful for me to study other modules, such as complex analysis and real analysis.

I have to declare that much of content in this course are borrowed from the thesis by Harris (2015). You could google her work with the reference I provied or just download it here.

To understand the convergence of Fourier series well, one needs to know Hilbert space first. In this book, Stein (2011) only introduces and explained the theory of Hilbert briefly, which makes me feel difficult to follow. Therefore, I would follow the thesis by Harris (2015) and examin the structure of Hilbert space first, which it will lay the foundation for understanding the convergence of Fourier series.
