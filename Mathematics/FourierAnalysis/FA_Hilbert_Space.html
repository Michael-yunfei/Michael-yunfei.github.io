Hilert Spaces And Some Examples

Definition
Let \(H\) be a complex vector space. An inner product on \(H\) is a function \( \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle : H \times H \to \mathbb{C} \), such that:

  1. \( \langle a x + by, z \rangle = a \langle x, z \rangle + b \langle y , z \rangle \) i.e. \(x \to \langle x, z \rangle \) is linear.
  2. \(\overline{\langle x, y \rangle} = \langle y, x \rangle \).
  3. \( ||x||^2 \equiv \langle x, y \rangle \geq 0 \) with equality \( ||x||^2 = 0 \) iff \( x = 0 \).
Notice tht combining properties (i) and (ii) that \(x \to \langle z, x \rangle \) is anti-linear for fixed \(z \in H \), i.e. \begin{aligned} \langle z, ax + by \rangle = \overline{\langle ax + by, z \rangle} & = \overline{a \langle x, z \rangle + b \langle y, z \rangle} \\ & = \overline{a \langle x , zy \rangle} + \overline{b \langle y, z \rangle} \ \ \ \text{(Complex conjugate operator is linear)} \\ & = \bar{a} \langle z, x \rangle + \bar{b} \langle z, y \rangle \end{aligned} We will often find the following formula useful: $$ \begin{aligned} ||x + y ||^2 = \langle x + y, x + y \rangle & = ||x ||^2 + ||y||^2 + \langle x, y \rangle + \langle y, x \rangle \\ & = ||x||^2 + ||y||^2 + \langle x, y \rangle + \overline{\langle x, y \rangle} \\ & = ||x||^2 + ||y||^2 + 2\text{Re}\langle x, y \rangle \end{aligned} $$

Theorem (Schwarz Inequality)
Let \( H, \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle \) be an inner product space, then for all \(x, y \in H \), we have: $$ |\langle x, y \rangle | \leq ||x || ||y || $$ and equality holds iff \(x \) and \(y\) are linearly dependent.
Proof. If \(y = 0 \), the result holds trivially. So assume that \(y \neq 0 \). First off notice that if \(x = \alpha y \) for some \(\alpha \in \mathbb{C} \), then \( \langle x , y \rangle = \alpha ||y ||^2 \) and hence $$ | \langle x, y \rangle | = |\alpha | ||y||^2 = ||x|| ||y||$$ Now suppose that \(x \in H \) is arbitrary, let \( z \equiv x - \frac{\langle x, y \rangle y}{||y||^2} \) (So \(z\) is the orthogonal projection of \(x \) onto \(y \) ) Then \begin{aligned} 0 \leq ||z||^2 & = \left| \left | x - \frac{\langle x, y \rangle}{||y||^2} y \right |\right|^2 = ||x ||^2 + \frac{|\langle x, y \rangle |^2}{||y||^4} ||y||^2 - 2 \text{Re} \langle x, \frac{\langle x, y \rangle}{||y||^2} y \rangle \\ & = ||x||^2 - \frac{|\langle x, y \rangle|^2}{||y||^2} \\ \end{aligned} from which it follows that $$0 \leq ||x||^2 ||y||^2 - |\langle x, y \rangle|^2 $$ with equality iff \( z = 0 \) or equivalently iff \(x = \frac{\langle x, y \rangle y}{||y||^2} \). \( \blacksquare \)
